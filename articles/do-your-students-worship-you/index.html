Do Your Students Worship You?

 

What loving empowerment looks like

in the classroom, for those that need it the most!

 

I am not a fan of categories, but for the sake of this article, I am going to make some generalizations.  Let's say there are two types of students.  One group of students who have supportive, loving homes.  The other group either have inconsistent support and/or basically, little to no nurturing at home.   Whether this lack of unconditional love was the result of trauma or some kind of dysfunction within the family unit, simply put, it has not been an easy road for this child or young adult.  

 

The second group are your students that either display signs that they are desperate for your love and attention, OR, they have shut down their heart and appear cold.  Either way, if you are a teacher that is in touch with your students, you will recognize these signs quickly. At that point, many of us give them what they do not have. Nurturing love.  It feels like our calling to love all of our children, especially those that come from broken places. So, we jump right in and begin to love that child back to life.  

 

What happens next, is extremely predictable and understandable!  It will not be long before your student begins to see you as a GOD.  When we are so thirsty, we will drink whatever is offered to us because we are desperate to find relief.  So, the moment this student lets you into their heart, the worship factor begins.  Can you imagine never having someone look at you with love in their eyes; or if you have never had your voice and presence in this world valued?  So of course, the moment someone *genuinely cares for you, you want to cling to them with your life.  It feels good to be authentically loved.  We all know this is true.

 

As a teacher, this can be both wonderful experience and a slippery slope.  The wonderful part is quite obvious.  You have accomplished your goal of reaching your student, on a heart level.  They know you love and care for them and it feels so good!  But, here is the slope:  when a student worships you to an extreme, they are externalizing love and not internalizing it.  A student can genuinely love you, respect you and look up to you as their mentor without making you into a God.  But, the students who have been starved of this attention, can easily swing to the other extreme.

 

So now, at this very point, YOU have the opportunity to make an incredibly positive impact.  Consider sitting with this child, one-on-one.  Acknowledge how much you love and value them.  Then, ask them, what does it feel like when you hear me say that in your body?  Most students, depending on their age, will say something like they are receiving a warm hug without anyone touching them.  Then consider sharing, in age-appropriate terms, that the warm hug they feel has always been there, inside of them their whole life!  It had just been lost and it was an honor to help them find it again.  The secret is, if you feel love from me and love towards me, that it must mean, it was already there inside of you!  Otherwise, it would have been unrecognizable to you.   It may feel like I put it there or it is because of me that you feel this hug.  Gently say, that hug you feel from me is actually YOU.  I just happen to notice you had lost it; because I love myself, I can feel it when someone else does not.  All I did was to help you bring back a part of you that was missing or blocked.  

 

As a teacher, when we do this, we are holding up a mirror and reminding our student who they really are...lovable, valuable and worth it!  The goal is for the student to realize that are worthy of being purely loved, just because they were born.  Holding up the mirror is literally shifting perceptions from external to internal; from overidentification of you, to an internal love of themselves.  This is a feeling they can access at any time, without our presence.  This will hopefully plant a seed, in the child, of internal empowerment.  The real joy is loving yourself.  When this happens, our cup is full and we can share that love in a healthy and authentic manner.  

 

Your students will respect you more, in the long run, for helping them find that lost piece of themselves.  My gut also tells me they will pay it forward, just like you do, every day in your classroom!  What could be more powerful than that?
